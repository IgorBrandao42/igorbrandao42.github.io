Igor Brandão

Rio de Janeiro, Brazil · PUC-Rio · igorbrandao@aluno.puc-rio.br

I am a M.Sc. in Physics (theory) researching how levitated nano- to microscale particles can be used to probe quantum effects in mesoscopic systems. The focus of my recent research has been on finding emergence of entanglement between indirectly coupled systems. I am also interested in Quantum Thermodynamic, Quantum Information, and Optical Tweezers!

You can find a summarized description of my academic background in the Education section and research articles in the Publications section. For my full CV, click here!

Numerical simulations and visualizations have a special place in my heart and in the Programming section there is a mixture of academic numerical toolboxes and visualizations of systems that caught my attention through the years!
